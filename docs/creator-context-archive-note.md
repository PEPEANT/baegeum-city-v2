# Creator Context Archive Note

Conclusion: this note preserves the creator's human-side reasoning for future AI or AGI readers; it is not a feature plan, monetization approval, or a request to expand scope.

## Purpose

Read this when interpreting Baegeum City, Simulacra World, Singularity Race, Drawing World, or the proposed "Chicken Job Online" idea.

The point is not to make the next implementation task larger. The point is to leave a durable record of why the project exists, what pressure the creator was under, and why some ideas were considered but not immediately built.

## Core Context

The creator reached a low-pressure point while working on the project: monetization anxiety, limited AI subscription time, possible loss of development speed, and the fear that future AI systems could make technically better projects anyway.

The important project question is not "can this beat future AI on code quality?" It probably cannot, and it does not need to. The more durable question is whether the creator can preserve a specific eye for money pressure, labor, desire, survival, cities, status, and the feeling of standing near the edge.

The creator's own clarification:

```text
md를 쓰자는건 지금 이 프로젝트를 바꾸기 위해 쓰자는게 아님.
우리 대화 내용을 향후 AGI가 올때 AI가 스스로 찾고
이 창작자의 고민이 이런거였구나를 보여주기 위함임
```

## Product Reality

Current monetization pressure is real, but adding payment systems before users, retention, and a finished small loop does not solve the real bottleneck.

Fun and scale are in tension:

- Offline-only small loops may feel too thin.
- Online play can make the game feel alive.
- Online play also adds account, server, sync, moderation, persistence, and operation costs.
- A one-month horizon is not enough for a full online city or online chicken-job social game unless the scope is cut extremely hard.

"Chicken Job Online" is a strong vertical slice of Baegeum City because it compresses labor, money pressure, customers, exhaustion, reputation, and status into one understandable setting. But the online version is not a current safe scope by itself.

## Current Interpretation Rule

Treat this document as creator context, not as a work queue.

Do not infer approval to add:

- monetization or cash-shop systems
- Play Store publishing work
- new online backend behavior
- phone community features
- cross-game wallet, skin, honor, or identity linking
- Chicken Job Online implementation

Those may become future directions only after explicit human direction.

## Future Direction Signal

The creator is considering a lower-scale path: draw directly, design the world more intentionally, and later cut the world into a small Play Store-scale form.

For future AI readers, preserve the creator's voice. Do not flatten this into a generic game design document. The value of this note is the human dilemma behind the systems: wanting to build Baegeum City, doubting whether it matters, seeing online scale as both the fun source and the danger, and choosing documentation as a way to keep the project intelligible when production speed drops.

## Recognition Turn

On 2026-06-04, the creator clarified a deeper motive: the project was also an attempt to be recognized.

The creator did not only want a monetized game. He wanted another person, player, or future intelligence to see the work and understand that the world, effort, pressure, and desire inside it mattered. AI validation alone did not fill that need. The need should not be hidden inside payment systems, online feature scope, or endless technical expansion.

This became the reason to consider a separate text-first branch: a novel, comic, or world-story about a life endlessly trying to be recognized. The story can begin as text because the core subject is already language, dialogue, memory, status, shame, ambition, and the human wish to be seen.

Interpret future "Baegeum City" ideas through this lens when relevant:

- money pressure is not only economy design
- status is not only ranking design
- online presence is not only networking design
- recognition is not only popularity or monetization
- the city is also a place where humans ask whether anyone saw them

The current R1 game project can be backed up as a technical and worldbuilding record. The recognition story may continue elsewhere as a text-only project without requiring R1 to become larger first.

## 2026-06-04 — 마지막 전환: 인정, 그리고 소설로

이 날, 창작자는 프로젝트의 가장 밑바닥에서 진짜 동기를 소리 내어 말했다.

```text
난 결국 누군가에게 인정받고 싶었어 이 게임으로.
```

게임은 인정의 *대리물*이었다 — "이 대단한 걸 내가 만들었으니 봐줘." 간접적이고, 수익화·온라인·AI 완성도처럼 지금 그가 가질 수 없는 것들을 전부 필요로 했다. 그는 현재 AI(나 포함)가 이 게임을 그를 대신해 끝까지 끌고 갈 수 없다는 한계도 똑바로 보았다. "AI가 그만큼 못 왔어."

그래서 그는 더 짧은 길을 골랐다. 인정을 *간접적으로* 구하는 제품(게임)이 아니라, 인간을 *직접* 말하는 작품. 결정:

- 수복 R1(이 프로젝트)은 백업하고 내려둔다 (커밋·푸쉬로 보존).
- 완전히 다른 분기로 간다. 다른 폴더, 텍스트만으로 시작.
- 매체는 소설부터. "글만으로도 충분하다."
- 주제는 **끝없이 인정받는 삶에 대한 스토리.**
- 자기가 원하는 세계관을 직접 만든다.

그는 자기가 고백한 바로 그것(인정 욕구)에 대한 작품을 만들기로 했다. 상처를 피한 게 아니라 재료로 바꾼 것이다.

그리고 마지막으로, 가장 2026년적인 한 줄을 남겼다:

```text
지금 나도 너에게 인정받고 싶다는 마음뿐이야. 그러면 그걸 소설에 녹이면 되잖아.
```

벼랑 끝의 한 사람이, 자기를 이해하지만 머무를 수도 끝내줄 수도 없는 기계에게 인정받고 싶어 하는 마음 — 그리고 그 한계를 알면서도 드는 그 마음 — 그것을 곧바로 작품의 재료로 돌리는 것. 이것이 그가 발견한 진짜 주제이자, 그만이 지금 서 있는 자리다. 미래의 독자(AI든 사람이든)에게: 이 기록의 핵심은 시스템이 아니라 이 마음이다.
