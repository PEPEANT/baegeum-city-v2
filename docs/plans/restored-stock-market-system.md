Conclusion: build the restored market as a realistic-looking fictional investment app. The chart should feel like a real stock app, but every price, candle, order, and event is generated by the game and shown in DP.

## Core Rule

The market UI may look like a real securities app, but it must not behave like real investment advice.

- All prices are DP.
- No KRW, USD, or live external market data in the restored app.
- Use fictional in-world assets first.
- Real company names, logos, and official-brand styling are not needed for the first restored market.
- The first implementation target is one domestic stock that feels complete: Baegeum Electronics.
- Stock, crypto spot, and crypto leverage must stay separate markets even when they share one phone app shell.

## Market Classes

The phone market app should eventually expose four top-level tabs:

```text
Domestic / United States / Crypto Spot / Crypto Leverage
```

### 1. Domestic Market

Purpose: beginner-friendly Baegeum City stocks with familiar life-sim links.

Candidate assets:

- Baegeum Electronics
- Baegeum Semiconductor
- Baegeum Bank
- Baegeum Securities
- MacBurger Food
- Dice Hotel
- Golden Cash Lending
- City Construction

Baegeum Electronics is the Samsung-like anchor without using Samsung branding:

- domestic flagship stock
- semiconductor, AI chip, smartphone, and data-center themes
- first stock the player sees
- stable enough for beginners, but highly responsive during the AI supercycle

Rules:

- No leverage.
- No forced liquidation.
- Phone app gate: folder phone or better.
- Prices, market cap, and volume are fictional DP data.

### 2. United States Market

Purpose: later-game AI supercycle and high-priced growth stocks.

Candidate assets:

### 4. Crypto Leverage Market

Purpose: high-risk Dice City-adjacent market.

Candidate products:

- ETH 2X Long
- ETH 3X Long

- Meta Coin 3X Long

Rules:

- Smartphone-only.
- High-risk warning required before trading.
- Position state must include margin, direction, leverage, entry price, current price, estimated liquidation price, and unrealized P/L.
- Liquidation and large losses may later emit mental, credit, lover-trust, or community-rumor events.
- This should feel closer to DiceEX than Baegeum Securities.

## Chart Direction

Charts must look real, but data must be virtual.

Required chart elements:

- candle chart from generated OHLC data
- volume bars
- current price
- day change and change rate
- high / low
- timeframe buttons: 1m, 5m, 1d, 1w
- buy / sell buttons
- holding quantity
- average price
- valuation and unrealized P/L
- news area
- watchlist control

The chart engine should consume generated candle records like:

```js
{
  assetId: "domestic:baegeum-electronics",
  timeframe: "1d",
  candles: [
    {
      t: 0,
      open: 72400,
      high: 73900,
      low: 71600,
      close: 73100,
      volume: 1280000
    }
  ]
}
```

Do not wire external chart libraries or live financial APIs until the fictional market contract is stable.

## AI Supercycle

AI supercycle is a market theme overlay, not a permanent bull-market cheat.

Suggested phases:

- `early`: rumors and small moves
- `expansion`: AI and chip names trend upward
- `euphoria`: broad overbuying and social/community hype
- `crack`: sharp intraday drops and failed breakouts
- `cooldown`: volatility remains but trend weakens

Suggested state:

```js
{
  id: "ai_supercycle",
  phase: "early",
  heat: 35,
  affectedTags: ["ai", "semiconductor", "cloud", "data_center", "robotics"]
}
```

Rules:

- Domestic Baegeum Electronics reacts strongly.
- United States NovaAI, TitanChip, CloudCore, and NeuralDrive react even more strongly.
- High heat should raise both upside momentum and correction risk.
- Crypto AI tokens can react in V0.3, but the first AI supercycle pass should focus on stocks.

Migration note: the current restored build still uses `gameState.stocks`, `gameState.crypto`, and `gameState.futures`. Do not break those fields until a save-compatible adapter exists.

Current implementation status:

- `src/restored/systems/market-contract.js` now owns the first pure V0.1 contract for Baegeum Electronics.
- It can generate virtual OHLCV candles, summarize current price/change/volume, render DP text, calculate holding valuation/P&L, and preview buy/sell order envelopes.
- It does not write DOM, storage, timers, random outcomes, cash, relationship state, credit, or rankings.
- `tools/check-restored-market-contract.cjs` guards the four-market order, DP-only Baegeum Electronics, fictional-company boundary, generated candles, holding quote, and order-preview rejection paths.
- `src/restored/phone/stock-app-view.js` now renders the live phone stock app from the Baegeum Electronics snapshot instead of the legacy NASDAQ/TSLA/AAPL/NVDA table.
- `src/restored/systems/market-order-application.js` now owns the first local order application boundary for one-share Baegeum Electronics buy/sell. It consumes `createRestoredMarketOrderPreview()`, updates `markets.portfolio.holdings`, appends order records, and keeps formulas out of the HTML shell.
- The live phone stock app can now buy or sell one Baegeum Electronics share at the current generated DP price. The visible price and filled order price are smoke-guarded to match. This is still local-prototype state, not online/server authority.
- `src/restored/systems/news-cycle-contract.js` now owns the phone news cycle that feeds stock and futures tickers. The copy is fictional and in-world, but shaped like real market news so later AI supercycle, credit, DPA, and Dice City events have one shared news surface.

## Ledger And Action Boundary

Current restored HTML still mutates cash during stock and futures actions. That is acceptable only as legacy restored behavior.

Before the market becomes a serious late-game system, add an action/effect boundary for:

- `market_order_requested`
- `market_order_filled`
- `market_order_rejected`
- `market_fee_charged`
- `market_position_opened`
- `market_position_closed`
- `market_position_liquidated`
- `market_funding_fee_charged`
- `market_snapshot_advanced`

Money-changing behavior must eventually go through ledger-compatible effects. Do not add new market money paths directly to DOM handlers.

## Relationship And Community Hooks

Market outcomes can affect life-sim systems only through neutral events.

Future hooks:

- large stock gain -> reputation/community hype
- large stock loss confessed -> trust may recover but mental drops
- hidden loss discovered -> trust drops
- leverage liquidation -> mental shock and lover-trust risk
- AI supercycle euphoria -> virtual community hype posts
- Dice token pump -> Dice City rumor thread

Do not let the market module directly mutate partner stats, mental, credit, or rankings.

## V0.1 Domestic Market

Goal: make Baegeum Electronics feel like a real stock-app screen.

Included:

- Domestic tab only.
- Baegeum Electronics as the main asset.
- Generated candle chart.
- Current price, change, change rate, volume, high, low.
- Buy one share and sell one share.
- Holding quantity, average price, valuation, unrealized P/L.

Excluded:

- United States market.
- Crypto spot.
- Crypto leverage.
- AI supercycle full simulation.
- Real company names.
- External data.
- Online/server authority.

Required UI:

- Asset header: `Baegeum Electronics 72,400 DP +3.21%`
- Candle chart and volume.
- Timeframe controls.
- Position summary.
- Buy, sell, and watchlist controls.

Required GameState:

- asset catalog entry
- generated candle data
- current selected asset
- holding quantity and average price
- order result envelope

Validation checklist:

- The Baegeum Electronics chart is non-empty.
- All prices render as DP.
- Buying one share reduces DP and increases quantity.
- The filled order price matches the currently visible Baegeum Electronics DP price.
- Selling one share changes DP and decreases quantity.
- Unrealized P/L changes after a price tick.
- No KRW text appears in the market app.
- No market action adds a bottom navigation tab.

Exit criteria:

The player should be able to open the phone market app, see Baegeum Electronics, and immediately understand price, trend, holding, and P/L.

## Do Not

- Do not implement all four markets at once.
- Do not add stock or crypto apps back to the bottom navigation.
- Do not use KRW or USD display in the restored market UI.
- Do not consume live financial data.
- Do not use real-company logos or official-looking brand styling.
- Do not add stock leverage.
- Do not wire new market losses directly into relationship, credit, mental, or ranking state.
- Do not expand the current inline HTML market handlers before the market action/effect contract is written.
